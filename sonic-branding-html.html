Sonic Branding: Interactive Guide

What is Sonic Branding?

Sonic branding (also called audio branding) is how companies use sound to represent their brand. It includes "every single sound a company makes that can be heard, wherever that is."

"Audio branding is defined as the structured integration of acoustic elements into a brand and its identity. Beyond a sonic logo, it encompasses the systematic establishment of a business' entire audio language for its brand based on its essence, vision, values promise, and personality."

Sonic branding includes:

Sonic Logos

Short sound clips that represent the brand

Jingles

Short musical pieces with or without words

Store Music

Background music and sounds in physical locations

Voice Guidelines

How people should speak when representing the brand

Why Sonic Branding Matters

  • Better brand recognition
  • Stronger memory of the brand
  • Creates emotional connections
  • Cuts through visual noise
  • Influences consumer behavior

What Makes Good Sonic Branding?

1. Match Your Brand Identity

Your sounds should match:

  • What your brand believes in (values)
  • How your brand would act if it were a person (personality)
  • Who your brand wants to talk to (audience)

"To figure out the soul and the backbone of the brand."

2. Create a Strong Sonic Logo

A good sonic logo is:

  • Easy to Remember: Simple enough to recall after hearing once or twice
  • Different: Not like other brands' sounds
  • Flexible: Can work in different situations and on different devices
  • Short: Ideally under two seconds
  • True to Brand: Sounds like what your brand represents

3. Be Strategic and Consistent

For sonic branding to work well:

  • It should match your visual brand elements
  • Use it the same way across all places people encounter your brand
  • Only use sound where people expect to hear sound

What Makes Sonic Branding Effective?

  • Sonic elements should be short and memorable
  • Each sound should have a distinctive character that fits the brand's identity
  • Sounds should be used consistently across all touchpoints
  • Sonic elements should work across multiple platforms and contexts
  • They should align with the visual identity of the brand
